# Rejecting Utilitarian Vegan Advocacy: A Guide for True Abolitionist Messaging

## Purpose

Many well-intentioned vegans who believe they are advocating for total liberation unknowingly reinforce utilitarian, welfarist messaging in their advocacy. They genuinely believe they are standing against all exploitation, abuse, and killing of non-human animals. However, their language, focus, and engagement with distractions undermine their intended message and allow oppressive frameworks to persist.

This guide is for those who already reject "humane" labels and incremental reforms but may still be unintentionally supporting utilitarian, plant-based welfarism by engaging in harm reduction debates, prioritising human benefit, and failing to centre non-human animals as individuals whose autonomy is systematically denied.

**True abolitionist messaging does not entertain distractions.** It dismantles the root ideology that sees non-human animals as commodities rather than autonomous individuals.

---

## The Core Problem: Utilitarian Framing in "Abolitionist" Discourse

Even among those who reject welfarist reforms, many still argue within the utilitarian framework rather than dismantling it entirely. They fail to attack the core belief that non-human animals exist for human use and instead focus on:

- Rejecting humane labels, but still framing advocacy around suffering reduction instead of non-human animals' right to exist freely.
- Calling for total abolition, but wasting energy debunking irrelevant distractions like "plants feel pain" instead of dismantling the ideology of animal ownership.
- Saying animal exploitation is inherently unjust, but allowing conversations about "convenience" or "health" to derail the message.

---

## How Utilitarian Thinking Weakens Vegan Advocacy

### 1. Arguing About Suffering Instead of Dismantling Domination

❌ Utilitarian framing: "Factory farms are the problem because they cause so much suffering."
✅ Abolitionist framing: "No system that enslaves, commodifies, and kills sentient beings is just. Whether suffering is extreme or minimal, the entire system is morally indefensible."

❌ Utilitarian framing: "Pigs are as smart as toddlers, so they shouldn't be killed."
✅ Abolitionist framing: "The issue isn't intelligence — it's that no sentient being should be bred, controlled, or killed as a resource."

❌ Utilitarian framing: "Chickens suffer the most in factory farms, so that should be our focus."
✅ Abolitionist framing: "There is no ethical difference between killing a chicken, a cow, or a pig. The issue is the belief that any of them can be property in the first place."

**Why This Matters:**
- Suffering can be reduced without ending oppression. But a more comfortable form of enslavement is still enslavement.
- Intelligence hierarchies reinforce human supremacy rather than dismantling it.
- Would we argue that slavery is wrong because enslaved people suffered, or because it was an inherently violent system of ownership?

---

### 2. Entertaining Distractions Instead of Staying on Message

❌ Common mistake: "Plants don't have a central nervous system, so they can't feel pain."
✅ Better: "This is a distraction. Even if plants could feel pain, how does that justify breeding, enslaving, and killing sentient beings?"

❌ Common mistake: "Farming plants kills fewer animals than farming animals."
✅ Better: "The issue is not numbers — it's the systemic commodification of sentient beings."

❌ Common mistake: "Factory farms are worse than small farms because they cause more suffering."
✅ Better: "A 'smaller cage' is still a cage. The issue isn't the conditions — it's the belief that non-human animals can be owned, exploited, and killed for profit."

**Why This Matters:**
- These distractions shift focus from oppression and autonomy to irrelevant hypotheticals.
- Justice is not about reducing numbers — it's about ending the entire system of domination.

---

### 3. Allowing Discussions to Centre Human Benefit

❌ Common mistake: "Veganism is the healthiest diet."
✅ Better: "Veganism isn't about human health. It's about rejecting the commodification, exploitation, and killing of sentient beings."

❌ Common mistake: "Veganism helps the environment."
✅ Better: "Even if animal agriculture were sustainable, it would still be unjust because it treats living beings as commodities."

❌ Common mistake: "Eating less meat is a step in the right direction."
✅ Better: "We wouldn't tell people to 'buy fewer slaves' as a step in the right direction. The only moral position is total abolition."

**Why This Matters:**
- If the argument centres on human benefits, the focus remains on self-interest rather than justice.
- Justice is not about convenience, sustainability, or health — it is about rejecting systems of control and violence.

---

### 4. Using Weak, Passive Language Instead of Absolute Messaging

❌ Common mistake: "Try to go vegan."
✅ Better: "Reject all animal exploitation. There is no ethical justification for violence, abuse, or killing."

❌ Common mistake: "Eating plant-based is a great way to help animals."
✅ Better: "Veganism is the bare minimum standard of rejecting oppression."

❌ Common mistake: "We should transition away from animal farming."
✅ Better: "No system that enslaves, abuses, and kills sentient beings should be reformed. It must be abolished."

---

## Shifting Toward Uncompromising Abolitionist Messaging

✔ **Do not entertain distractions.** Keep the focus on exploitation, abuse, and oppression — not hypotheticals.
✔ **Do not argue within the utilitarian framework.** Do not validate harm reduction — demand total rejection.
✔ **Do not allow the conversation to centre human benefit.** This is about liberation, not convenience.
✔ **Use strong, direct, absolute language.** No "try," no "better options," no "less suffering." Abolition is the only moral stance.

---

## Final Thought

If your advocacy does not demand the total, immediate abolition of all non-human animal exploitation — without compromise, without distractions, and without appeals to human self-interest — then you are unintentionally validating oppression.

Reject the utilitarian trap. Stay on message. Demand abolition. No excuses. No distractions. No half-measures.
